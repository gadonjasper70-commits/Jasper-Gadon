Game Design Reference

1 · Objective & Win Condition

Empty your hand before your opponents by legally playing Logic Cards (matched by color or number, like UNO) and Action Cards. Playing a Logic Card requires correctly identifying the requested form — Conditional, Converse, Inverse, or Contrapositive — of a proposition drawn from the deck. In Practice Mode the "win" is simply completing a set of questions with a strong streak.

2 · Turn Sequence

  • Play a card from your hand that matches the discard pile's color, number, or is a Wild — Logic Cards trigger a proposition question first.
  • Answer correctly within the timer to land the card and pass the turn (respecting Skip/Reverse/+2 effects).
  • Answer incorrectly or time out: the card returns to your hand, you draw one penalty card, and your turn ends.
  • No playable card in hand: draw one from the pile. If it's playable you may play it immediately, otherwise your turn passes.

3 · Card & Deck System

Logic Cards (numbered 0–9, four colors): 🔴 Conditional, 🔵 Converse, 🟢 Inverse, 🟡 Contrapositive.

Action Cards: Skip (next player loses a turn), Reverse (flips play direction), Challenge +2 (target must answer a proposition in 10s or draw two), Wild (choose the next color), Wild Challenge (choose color + target answers a hard question or draws four).

Proposition Deck: a bank of everyday and mathematical statements tagged Easy / Medium / Hard, each with a programmatically generated answer key for all four forms.

4 · Scoring & Penalties

Correct answers award points scaled by difficulty and remaining time, plus a streak bonus. Wrong answers or timeouts reset the streak and add one penalty card to your hand. Practice Mode disables penalties and adds an optional hint after a miss.

5 · Modes

Practice — solo, untimed-friendly, hints on. Multiplayer — 2–4 players, hotseat pass-device privacy gate between turns. AI Mode — 1–3 bots with simulated accuracy based on difficulty. Teacher Mode — reveals a live answer-key panel and feeds every attempt into the Class Performance report.

6 · Design System

Each logic form owns a color used consistently across cards, legends, and charts. Type pairs a serif display face for headings with a plain body face and a monospace face for logic notation (p, q, ~p, ~q). Sound effects and a soft generative background pad are synthesized in-browser (Web Audio) and can be muted independently.

7 · Analytics

Per-player accuracy by form, current and best streak, and total score are tracked live. The Class Performance table aggregates every player across every session saved in this browser, exportable as JSON or CSV for a gradebook.

8 · Technical Notes

Architecture: single-file app split into DATA (proposition bank, deck factory), STATE (game object), AUDIO, STORAGE, RENDER, and GAME LOGIC modules. Bug prevention: all plays are validated against the current top card before mutating state, question modals disable double-submission, and timers are cleared on every transition. Save system: analytics persist via key-value storage scoped to this browser; matches themselves are session-only by design so every game starts clean. Future expansion: hooks are in place to add new proposition packs, extra action cards, or online multiplayer without touching the core turn engine.
